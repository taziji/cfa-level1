Derivatives
  • Derivative Concept
    • Framework
      • Classes of derivative
        • Forward commitment
          • Forward
            • Is an agreement that obligates one party to buy and the other party to sell a specific quantity of an underlying asset, at a set price, at a future date
            • A forward contract is a bilateral contract that obligate sone party to buy and the other party to sell a specific quantity of an underlying asset, at a set price, on a specific date in the future.
            • Types of forward contracts
              • Commodity forward contract
              • Financial forward contract
            • Purposes of trading forward contracts
              • Hedge risk: lock the cost in the future, but not sure to make money; have default risk.
              • Speculation: gambling the price movement.
            • Characteristics
              • Each party are exposed to default risk ( or counter party risk)
              • Zero-sum game
            • Difference between forward and future
          • Futures
            • Is a specialized version of a forward contract that has been standardized and that trades on a futures exchange.
            • Characteristics
              • A forward contract
              • Are regulated
              • Guarantee provided by the exchange through the clearinghouse
              • the daily settlement for gains and losses
            • Risk control of futures contract
              • Method 1: margin
                • Initial margin (Future 补交到IM，Equity补交到MM）
                • Maintenance margin
                  • If the margin balance is lower than the maintenance margin, the trader will get a margin call
                • Variation margin: used to bring the margin balance back up to the initial margin level (IM - $ in the account)
                • difference between equity margin
              • Method 2: daily price limit
              • Method 3: daily settlement （逐日盯市）
          • Swap
            • Is a series of forward contracts
            • Exchange a series of cash flows
            • Interest rate swaps: The plain vanilla interest rate swap involves trading fixed interest rate payments for floating-rate payment (paying fixed and receiving floating).
              • Counter parties: the parties involved in any swap agreement are called the counter parties
              • Pay-fixed side: the counter party that makes fixed-rate interest payment in exchange for variable interest rate.
              • Pay-floating side: the counter party that makes variable-rate interest payment in exchange for fixed payment.
        • Contingent claim
          • Option
            • The owner has the right, but not the obligation to conduct the transaction.
            • Right and obligations are not equal only in option contract, so the long position need to pay option premium.
            • Call option: long call & short call / Put option: long put & short put / writer of the option
            • Prices: Option premium / Exercise price (strike price)
            • Moneyness (Immediate exercise): on the long position
              • In the money: payoff > 0
              • At the money: payoff=0
              • Out of the money: payoff<0
              • Stock's current price, S and Option's exercise strike price, X
              • Payoff
              • Gain/Loss (考虑premium之后）
              • Option value
                • Intrinsic value:
                  • Intrinsic value of call option: c=max[0, S-X]
                  • Intrinsic value of put option: p=max[0, X-S]
                • Time value
                  • The difference between the price of an option (called its premium)and its intrinsic value is due to its time value
                • Option value=intrinsic value + time value
                  • Before expiration: option value>intrinsic value
                  • At expiration: option value=intrinsic value
                • Price of the option is more volatile than prices of underlying stock
            • Early Exercise of American Options (European Options cann't)
              • American call options (只有大规模分红会提前行权)
              • American put options (标的资产趋向于0会提前行权)
            • Factors affect the value of an option （记忆负相关和特例）
              • * There is an exception to the general rule that European put option thetas are negative. The put value may increases as the option approaches maturity if the option is deep in-the-money and close to maturity. （跌到0）
          • Credit default swap (CDS)

    • Advantage & Disadvantage (Not always increase the risk)
      • Advantage
        • Price discovery
        • Risk management: hedge and speculation
        • Lowering transaction costs
        • Low capital requirement
        • Greater liquidity
        • Ease of going short
        • Enhance market efficiency
      • Disadvantage
        • Too risky -> High leverage.
        • Complex instruments.
        • Sometimes likened to gambling.
    • Settlement
      • At expiration
        • Physical settlement: deliver an actual asset, has storage cost, mostly used in commodity forward.
        • Cash settlement: the party that has a position with negative value is obligated to pay that amount to the other party, mostly used in financial forward.
      • Prior to expiration
        • Entering into an opposite forward contract: with an expiration date equal to the time remaining on the original contract
          • Offsetting with a different party: some credit risk remains
          • Offsetting with the original party: can avoid credit risk
    • Forward Rate Agreements (FRA): a forward contract on an interest rate (LIBOR 作为标的)
      • LIBOR (London Interbank Offer Rate), Euribor, and FRAs
      • FRA can be viewed as a forward contract to borrow/lend money at a certain rate at some future date.
        • The long position: is the party that would borrow the money 担心利率上升
        • The short position: is the party that would lend the money 担心利率下跌
        • Quotation
    • Put call parity (CK=PS)
      • Fiduciary call (CK)
        • A long position in a European call option with an exercise price of X that maturities in T years on a stock.
        • A long position in a pure-discount riskless bond that pays X in T years.
      • Protective put
        • A long position in a European put option with an exercise price of X that maturities in T years on a stock.
        • A long position in the underlying stock.
      • Put – Call – Forward Parity
        • FP代入得到Put call parity
  • Derivative Pricing and Valuation
    • The price is the predetermined price in the contract that the long should pay to the short to buy the underlying asset at the settlement date （在衍生产品中定价定的是标的资产的价格）
      • The no-arbitrage principle (Law of one price. Pricing 遵循的原则)
      • a forward contract is the process of determining the no-arbitrage price that will make the value of the contract be zero to both sides at the initiation of the contract
      • Forward Price= price that would not permit profitable riskless arbitrage in frictionless markets
      • General equations (price)
      • Forward contracts on a dividend-paying stock/bond (cash flow are dividends and coupons)
      • T-bill (zero-coupon bond) forwards (no carrying cost and carrying benefit)
    • Valuation of a derivative contract means determining the payoff of the contract to the long (or short) position at some time during the life of the contract.
      • Risk neutrality (should yield the risk-free rate of return Valuation 遵循的原则)
      • General equations (valuation)
    • The net cost and benefit is often referred to by the term carry, or sometimes cost of carry. (Benefit-cost, γ-θ)
    • Prices of Futures vs. Forward contracts (r高， future由于逐日盯市可以再投资）
    • Swap Pricing and Valuation (Equivalence of swaps to bonds)
      • An interest rate swap is identical to issuing a fixed-rate bond and using the proceeds to buy a floating-rate bond.
    • Binomial model
      • The underlying price moves to two new prices at option expiration, where u=1/d
      • Risk-neutral probability
      • We start with a call option. If the stock goes up to S1+ , the call option willbe worth C1+. If the stock goes down to S1−, the call option will be worth C1−.
